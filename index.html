THE BASICS

Power washing, also called pressure washing, uses high-powered water to remove dirt, grime, and other surface build-ups. Power washers are typically used on homes and other buildings, but they can also be used on vehicles, decks, patios, etc.


WHAT IS POWER WASHING?

Power washing uses high-pressure water to remove dirt, grime, and other build-up from surfaces. It can be done with either a pressure washer or a power washer. Pressure washers use a pump to increase the pressure of the water, while power washers use an engine to do the same thing.


HOW PRESSURE WASHING WORKS

Pressure washing works by using high-pressure water to remove dirt, grime, and another build-up from surfaces. The high pressure of the water helps to loosen the build-up so that it can be easily removed. Pressure washers use water pressure to clean surfaces. They are typically gas- or electric-powered and can be used for various purposes, such as cleaning, washing cars, houses, decks, and sidewalks. The pressure of the water ejects dirt, dust, oil, grime, and other contaminants from the surface being cleaned. Pressure washers are a great way to keep your property looking clean and new without spending much time or money on manual labor.
